Tanga, Tanzania – Coastal Growth Hub of East Africa

Strategic Location | Energy Gateway | Trade & Tourism Potential

Tanga is rapidly emerging as one of East Africa's most dynamic regional hubs — blending logistics, energy, tourism, and transport infrastructure upgrades that are reshaping the city's economic landscape and long-term investment appeal.

Port Expansion and Trade Capacity

Tanga Port has been upgraded with a deepened and widened channel, reduced turnaround times, and enhanced cargo handling, boosting trade and reducing logistics costs. These improvements position Tanga as a regional maritime hub, also serving as the export point for oil via EACOP.

Transformative Energy Corridor: EACOP

The East African Crude Oil Pipeline connects Uganda's oilfields to Tanga, spanning 1,443 km. Over 80% complete, expected to start exports by late 2026. Once operational, it will be the world's longest electrically heated pipeline, transporting 230,000 barrels per day.

Transport Connectivity and Regional Mobility

Tanga's transport links are improving with planned upgrades to the airport, better rail connections, and new highways enhancing regional mobility and cross-border trade.
