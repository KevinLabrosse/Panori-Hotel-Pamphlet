Location Intelligence

Tanga, Tanzania —
Coastal Growth Hub of East Africa

Strategic Location | Energy Gateway | Trade & Tourism Potential

Port Expansion & Trade Capacity

Tanga Port has been upgraded with a deepened and widened channel, reduced turnaround times, and enhanced cargo handling, boosting trade and reducing logistics costs. These improvements position Tanga as a regional maritime hub, also serving as the export point for oil via EACOP.

EACOP — Transformative Energy Corridor

The East African Crude Oil Pipeline connects Uganda's oilfields to Tanga, spanning 1,443 km. Over 80% complete, expected to start exports by late 2026. Once operational, it will be the world's longest electrically heated pipeline, transporting 230,000 barrels per day.

Transport Connectivity & Regional Mobility

Tanga's transport links are improving with planned upgrades to the airport, better rail connections, and new highways enhancing regional mobility and cross-border trade.

Investment Case

Why Tanga, Why Now

Tourism & Island Access: Zanzibar & Pemba

Tanga's proximity to Zanzibar and Pemba makes it a prime coastal stop. Improved ferry services and tourism development — including nearby Amboni Caves, Saadani National Park, and beaches — are boosting its tourism appeal.

Growth Meets Lifestyle

Tanga combines opportunity, connectivity, and natural beauty. For investors targeting an early-mover advantage in a rising East African city, Tanga stands out as one of the best-positioned opportunities today — with growing economic momentum across real estate, hospitality, logistics, and tourism.
